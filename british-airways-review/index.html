British Airways Review Dashboard

An interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing customer satisfaction for British Airways from 2016 to 2023.

The Challenge: Understanding the Voice of the Customer

British Airways, like any global airline, faces the challenge of meeting the diverse needs of its customers. Travelers leave reviews that reflect their experiences, from praises for attentive staff to complaints about uncomfortable seats or poor in-flight entertainment. These reviews hold the key to understanding how the airline can improve, but the sheer volume of data makes it hard to pinpoint trends and insights.

This project was born from a desire to turn raw customer feedback into a clear, actionable story. Using Tableau, I created an interactive dashboard that uncovers the hidden patterns in customer reviews, helping British Airways make data-driven decisions.

The Journey: Building the Dashboard

To tell the story of British Airways' customer satisfaction, I analyzed reviews from March 2016 to October 2023, focusing on key metrics that travelers care about most:

  • Cabin staff service
  • Entertainment
  • Food and beverages
  • Seat comfort
  • Value for money

The result? An interactive dashboard that gives stakeholders the power to:

  • Spot trends over time.
  • Compare customer ratings across regions, traveler types, and seat classes.
  • Understand which aircraft models delight passengers—and which fall short.

The Insights: What We Discovered

1. Strengths: Where British Airways Shines

  • Cabin Staff Service: With an average rating of 3.3, passengers consistently praise the professionalism and friendliness of the airline’s crew.
  • Top-Performing Aircraft: The Boeing 747-400 emerges as a crowd favorite, scoring an impressive 4.7 in overall satisfaction.

2. Weaknesses: Areas That Need Attention

  • Entertainment: With an abysmal score of 1.4, passengers are clearly dissatisfied with in-flight entertainment options. Whether it's outdated screens or a lack of variety, this is a major area for improvement.
  • Food and Beverages: At 2.4, customers find the quality and variety of meals underwhelming.
  • Value for Money: A score of 2.8 reflects passengers feeling they aren’t getting what they pay for.

3. Regional Stories: Satisfaction Varies by Geography

  • High-Rated Regions: Certain countries consistently rate British Airways highly, suggesting strong service delivery or loyalty in those markets.
  • Low-Rated Regions: Lighter areas on the map reveal regions where passengers leave dissatisfied, possibly due to cultural mismatches or operational challenges.

4. Fleet Insights: The Role of Aircraft in Customer Satisfaction

  • Top Performers: The Boeing 747-400 and Boeing 787 deliver excellent experiences, with scores of 4.7 and 4.4, respectively.
  • Underperformers: The A321 lags behind with a score of 3.6, signaling issues with comfort or amenities that need to be addressed.

Interactive Exploration: Putting the Power in Your Hands

The dashboard isn’t just a static report—it’s a tool that allows stakeholders to explore the data in their own way. Want to know how business travelers feel about seat comfort? Or how family leisure travelers rate food and beverages? The interactive filters make it easy to dive deep:

  • Select a Metric: Focus on specific areas like entertainment, food, or seat comfort.
  • Adjust the Time Range: See how ratings have evolved over the years.
  • Filter by Traveler Type: Compare ratings for business travelers vs. solo leisure passengers.
  • Drill Down by Aircraft or Region: Pinpoint issues with specific planes or geographic markets.

The Impact: What This Means for British Airways

  • Invest in Entertainment: Upgrading in-flight entertainment systems is a clear opportunity to improve passenger satisfaction.
  • Improve Food Quality: Offering diverse, high-quality meal options can address one of the most common complaints.
  • Enhance Value for Money: Adjusting pricing or improving services can make passengers feel like they’re getting more for what they pay.
  • Focus on Fleet Performance: Investigating issues with low-rated aircraft (e.g., the A321) can help the airline prioritize improvements.
  • Address Regional Challenges: Tailoring services to underperforming regions can boost satisfaction and customer loyalty.

The Tools Behind the Story

  • Tableau: For creating the interactive dashboard and visualizing the data.
  • Data Analysis: Aggregating and cleaning customer review data to uncover trends.
  • Storytelling with Data: Highlighting actionable insights to drive business decisions.
