Customer Analysis Dashboard

The Customer Analysis Dashboard provides a detailed and interactive view of customer purchasing behavior, revenue generation, and sales patterns across various dimensions. This project showcases how businesses can derive actionable insights from data to understand their performance better, identify growth opportunities, and refine strategies.

This dashboard analyzes customer behavior across different categories, regions, genders, age groups, and time periods, all while offering the flexibility to dive deeper using dynamic filters. It is built to help businesses answer crucial questions such as:

  • Which product categories contribute the most to revenue?
  • How does revenue vary across regions and states?
  • What demographic segments are the primary contributors?
  • How do discounts affect quantity ordered?

The ability to filter by category, time period, and demographic details ensures that users can focus on specific segments and tailor their decision-making process.

Revenue by Categories

The dashboard breaks down revenue into 15 distinct product categories:

  • Appliances
  • Beauty & Grooming
  • Books
  • Computing
  • Entertainment
  • Health & Sports
  • Home & Living
  • Kids & Baby
  • Men’s Fashion
  • Mobiles & Tablets
  • Others
  • School & Education
  • Soghaat
  • Superstore
  • Women’s Fashion

The analysis shows that Mobiles & Tablets and Women’s Fashion dominate revenue, with substantial contributions from Appliances, Beauty & Grooming, and Superstore. Meanwhile, categories such as Books, Health & Sports, and Soghaat show potential for growth, highlighting opportunities for businesses to invest in underperforming segments.

Revenue per State

The Revenue per State Map visualizes how sales are distributed geographically across the United States. Using a heatmap, it highlights states with higher revenue contributions in darker shades of orange:

States such as California, Texas, and New York consistently generate high revenue. Smaller contributions are observed in less populous states such as Alaska and Vermont, which could indicate limited market penetration or a smaller customer base.

Monthly Revenue Trends

The Month-Wise Revenue Chart tracks sales performance over time, revealing peaks and troughs that highlight seasonal trends:

Revenue spikes are evident in December, driven by holiday shopping, and in April, potentially due to spring promotions. Periods like January and July show lower revenue, suggesting off-peak seasons.

Region-Wise Revenue Share

The Region-Wise Revenue Pie Chart divides total revenue by geographic regions, providing a quick snapshot of where sales are strongest:

The South leads with 38.37% of total revenue, followed by the Midwest (26.93%) and the West (17.60%). The Northeast lags slightly at 17.10%, offering opportunities to focus on customer engagement in this region.

Gender-Wise Sales Analysis

The Gender-Wise Revenue Bar Chart showcases how male and female customers contribute to revenue across different categories:

Female customers drive significant revenue in Women’s Fashion ($15.04M) and Beauty & Grooming ($1.37M). Male customers dominate in Mobiles & Tablets ($66.27M) and Appliances ($13.45M).

Age-Wise Revenue Analysis

The Age-Wise Revenue Bar Chart explains how different age groups contribute to total revenue. The 30-40 age group generates the highest revenue at $44.8M, followed by 60-70 years ($41.1M).

Quantity-Discount Correlation

The Scatter Plot visualizes the relationship between the discount percentage and the quantity ordered. A clear trend emerges, showing that higher discounts often lead to larger quantities being ordered.

Interactive Features

The dashboard’s interactive filters make it a versatile tool for in-depth analysis. Users can:

  • Select one or more categories to analyze revenue and purchasing behavior.
  • Filter by gender, age group, and region to explore demographic-specific insights.
  • Adjust the time range to focus on seasonal patterns or specific years.
